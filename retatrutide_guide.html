Complete Retatrutide (Reta) Starter Guide

For Educational & Research Use Only. This guide summarizes clinical trial protocols for Retatrutide and details proper storage and reconstitution procedures.

waving_hand Welcome

Welcome to your Retatrutide Starter Guide. This guide explains how retatrutide has been studied in clinical trials, how to prepare and store a lyophilized vial, and what to expect throughout treatment.

What is Retatrutide?

Retatrutide (often shortened to **Reta**) is a **once-weekly triple hormone receptor agonist** that targets:

  • GLP-1 (Glucagon-like peptide-1)
  • GIP (Glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide)
  • Glucagon

It is being studied for obesity, overweight, and metabolic disorders. Clinical trials have shown promising results, but the medication remains under investigation and an official commercial prescribing label has not yet been established.

Typical Clinical Trial Protocol

Most Phase 3 clinical trials introduced retatrutide gradually to improve tolerability.

Treatment Period Trial Dose*
Weeks 1–4 2 mg once weekly
Weeks 5–8 4 mg once weekly
Weeks 9–12 6 mg once weekly
Weeks 13–16 9 mg once weekly
Week 17 onward Up to 12 mg once weekly

*This reflects dosing schedules used in clinical trials and is **not an approved prescribing recommendation**. Your healthcare provider or research protocol may differ.

What to Expect

Some people experience temporary gastrointestinal side effects while their body adjusts, including:

Nausea
Reduced Appetite
Fullness Sooner
Constipation
Diarrhea

These symptoms often improve as treatment continues.

Choosing the Right Vial

Choose your vial based on your current weekly treatment plan, not the highest dose you may eventually reach.

If You're New to Retatrutide:

People following the Phase 3 trial schedule often begin with 2 mg once weekly for the first four weeks.

For this reason, many first-time users choose a vial that provides approximately one month's supply before purchasing larger quantities.

If You're Already on a Stable Weekly Amount:

Choose a vial size that closely matches approximately four weeks of your current weekly treatment plan to prevent excessive waste and manage shelf life.

Why Not Buy the Largest Vial?

Retatrutide is supplied as a **lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder** and must be reconstituted before use.

Once reconstituted, the vial has a recommended beyond-use period. Although a larger vial may contain several months' worth of medication based on your weekly amount, the mixed solution is not intended to be stored indefinitely.

Choosing a vial that closely matches your current treatment plan can help reduce waste while ensuring the product is used within the recommended storage period.

warning
Inspect before each use: Do not use the reconstituted solution if it appears cloudy, discolored, or contains unexpected floating particles.

How Long Will I Stay on Retatrutide?

Because retatrutide is still under investigation, there is no approved treatment duration. Clinical trials have evaluated treatment over many months, with regular follow-up and dose adjustments according to study protocols. Ongoing treatment decisions should always be made with your healthcare provider.
