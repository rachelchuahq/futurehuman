Peptide Safety Checklist & Disclaimer

Read Before You Use Any Peptide

warning

Peptides that are not approved by the FDA for human use have not been evaluated for safety, effectiveness, or manufacturing quality. Even if many people report positive experiences, there is always a risk that the product may be contaminated, mislabeled, underdosed, overdosed, or counterfeit.

Nothing on this checklist guarantees a peptide is safe. Its purpose is to help you reduce avoidable risks and recognize when something isn't right.

shopping_cart

Before You Buy

check_circle
Research the peptide and understand what it is intended to do.
check_circle
Ask for a batch-specific Certificate of Analysis (COA) showing:
  • Identity
  • Purity
  • Batch number
check_circle
Prefer suppliers that also provide:
  • Sterility testing
  • Endotoxin testing
  • Transparent manufacturing information
gpp_maybe
Be skeptical of:
  • Miracle claims
  • "Zero side effects"
  • Prices that seem too good to be true
  • Missing or outdated testing documents
science

Before You Use It

check_circle
Inspect the vial:
  • No cracks or damage
  • Powder should look normal (no unusual discoloration)
  • Solution should be clear after reconstitution (unless otherwise specified)
check_circle
Check: Expiration date, Batch number, and Storage requirements.
check_circle
Use sterile supplies every time.
cancel
Never use a vial if you suspect contamination.

Stop Immediately If You Notice

emergency Fever or chills after an injection
emergency Rash or hives
emergency Trouble breathing
emergency Chest pain
emergency Severe dizziness or fainting
emergency Persistent vomiting
emergency Increasing redness, warmth, swelling, or pus at the injection site
emergency Any symptom that feels severe or rapidly worsens
Seek medical attention immediately if these occur.

During Use

Pay attention to how your body responds. Watch for:

check_box_outline_blank Unusual swelling
check_box_outline_blank Persistent headaches
check_box_outline_blank Rapid heartbeat
check_box_outline_blank Significant changes in blood sugar (if applicable)
check_box_outline_blank Mood or sleep changes
check_box_outline_blank Unexpected pain or weakness

If symptoms continue or worsen, stop using the peptide and consult a healthcare professional.

Best Practices

done
Start with the lowest dose supported by available human research, if any exists.
done
Introduce only one new peptide at a time so you can identify the cause of side effects.
done
Keep a simple log: Date, Dose, Batch number, Time taken, and Any side effects.
done
Store peptides exactly as recommended by the manufacturer.
block
Never share needles, syringes, or opened vials.

Final Reminder

Every peptide carries some level of risk—especially those that are not approved for human use. No online review, influencer recommendation, or Certificate of Analysis can completely guarantee safety.

The best protection is staying informed, buying only from transparent sources, using proper sterile technique, monitoring your body's response, and seeking medical care if something doesn't seem right.
