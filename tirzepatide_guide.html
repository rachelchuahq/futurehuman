Complete Tirzepatide Starter Guide

For Educational & Research Use Only. This guide summarizes standard prescribing recommendations and is intended to outline the proper handling, scheduling, and storage of Tirzepatide.

waving_hand Welcome

Welcome to your Tirzepatide Starter Guide. This guide is designed to help you understand how tirzepatide is typically introduced, prepared, and used. Whether you're starting your first injection or returning for a refill, this guide serves as a quick reference throughout your treatment.

Always follow the treatment plan provided by your prescribing healthcare provider.

What is Tirzepatide?

Tirzepatide is a once-weekly injectable medication that is introduced gradually using a dose-escalation schedule. Starting with a lower dose allows the body to adjust before higher doses are considered, which may help reduce gastrointestinal side effects.

Typical Starting Protocol

Most healthcare providers follow the schedule below. Starting at 2.5 mg helps minimize stomach side effects before progressing to maintenance doses.

Treatment Period Typical Weekly Dose*
Weeks 1–4 2.5 mg once weekly
Weeks 5–8 5 mg once weekly (if appropriate)
Every 4 weeks thereafter May increase by 2.5 mg if needed and well tolerated
Maximum recommended dose 15 mg once weekly

*This reflects the standard prescribing recommendations. Your healthcare provider may recommend a different schedule based on your individual treatment plan.

event_repeat Your Weekly Routine

check_circle

Weekly Administration

Inject once every week on the same day each week.

check_circle

Rotate Injection Sites

Rotate injection sites between the abdomen, thigh, or upper arm.

check_circle

Hydration & Nutrition

Stay thoroughly hydrated. Eating smaller meals may help if stomach discomfort occurs.

warning If You Miss a Dose

Within 4 days (96 hours): Administer the missed dose as soon as possible.
More than 4 days: Skip the missed dose and continue with your next scheduled injection.
dangerous Warning: Do not administer two doses within 72 hours of one another.

What to Expect

Some people experience mild side effects during the first few weeks while their body adjusts. These effects are often temporary and improve over time.

Choosing the Right Vial

Choose your vial based on your current prescribed weekly dose, not the highest dose you may eventually reach.

If You're New to Tirzepatide:

Most people begin with the recommended introductory schedule of 2.5 mg once weekly for the first four weeks.

For this reason, many first-time users choose a 10 mg vial, which typically provides approximately one month's supply.

If You're Already on a Stable Dose:

Use the guide below to estimate a vial that provides approximately 4 weeks of treatment:

Why Not Buy the Largest Vial?

Tirzepatide is supplied as a **lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder** and must be reconstituted before use.

Once reconstituted, the vial has a recommended beyond-use period. Although a larger vial may contain several months' worth of medication based on your weekly dose, the mixed solution is not intended to be stored for that entire period.

Choosing a vial that closely matches your current treatment plan can help reduce waste while ensuring you're using the product within the recommended storage timeframe. Once your maintenance dose has been established, you can decide whether a larger vial size better suits your ongoing treatment.

kitchen Storage Guidelines

ac_unit Before Reconstitution

  • Store the unopened lyophilized vial according to the product storage instructions.
  • When stored correctly, the vial remains usable until its labeled expiration date.

refrigerator After Reconstitution

  • REFRIGERATE after use.
  • Follow the recommended beyond-use period after reconstitution.
  • The amount of medication remaining in the vial does not extend its recommended use period.
warning
Inspect before each use: Do not use the reconstituted solution if it appears cloudy, discolored, or contains unexpected floating particles.

How Long Will I Stay on Tirzepatide?

There is no predetermined treatment duration. Many people continue tirzepatide as an ongoing weekly therapy while regularly reviewing their progress with their healthcare provider. Your treatment duration and maintenance dose should always be determined by your prescribing healthcare provider.
