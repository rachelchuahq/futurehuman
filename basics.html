science Are peptides steroids?
expand_more

No. Peptides are not steroids. They are short chains of amino acids—the same building blocks that make up proteins. Steroids and peptides work very differently and belong to different classes of compounds.

gavel Are all peptides FDA approved?
expand_more

No. While a small number of peptide-based medicines have been approved by the U.S. FDA for specific medical conditions, many peptides sold for laboratory research have not been approved by the FDA. Research peptides are intended for scientific investigation and are not approved for human consumption.

forum If people talk about peptides online, does that mean they're proven to work?
expand_more

Not necessarily. Social media, podcasts, and online discussions often share personal experiences, but these are not the same as scientific evidence. Reliable conclusions come from well-designed laboratory studies and clinical trials.

rule Why are these products labeled "For Research Use Only"?
expand_more

Research peptides have not completed the regulatory process required for FDA approval for human use. They are supplied for legitimate laboratory and scientific research and are not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.

verified_user Does "natural" mean a peptide is safe?
expand_more

No. Whether a compound occurs naturally or is made in a laboratory does not automatically make it safe or appropriate for human use. Safety and effectiveness can only be established through proper scientific research and regulatory review.

water_drop What is peptide reconstitution?
expand_more

Reconstitution is the process of adding a sterile liquid to a freeze-dried peptide so it becomes a solution for laboratory research. It changes the concentration of the solution but does not change the total amount of peptide in the vial.

opacity Does adding more liquid make the peptide stronger?
expand_more

No. Adding more or less liquid only changes the concentration of the solution. The total amount of peptide in the vial stays exactly the same.

ac_unit Why do peptides come as a powder?
expand_more

Many peptides are freeze-dried to help improve stability during storage and shipping. They are typically reconstituted before use in laboratory research.

calculate Is your peptide calculator a dosing guide?
expand_more

No. The calculator is a mathematical tool only. It calculates concentration based on the amount of peptide and liquid entered. It does not provide medical advice, treatment recommendations, or guidance for human use.

school Why is Future Human focused on education?
