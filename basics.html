lightbulb Did You Know?

Your peptide vial may look almost empty because active peptides are measured in milligrams (mg)—the visible powder is often a freeze-dried cake structure.

bolt Stability Fact

Lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder peptides are highly stable at room temperatures during transit compared to already reconstituted liquid forms.

verified Handling Tip

Rolling a vial gently between your palms is the standard method to dissolve reconstituted powder; shaking vigorously can break peptide structures.

Frequently Asked Questions

Review instant answers to common questions about research peptides.

Are peptides steroids? expand_more
No. Peptides are not steroids. They are short chains of amino acids—the same building blocks that make up proteins. Steroids and peptides work very differently and belong to different classes of compounds.
Are all peptides FDA approved? expand_more
No. While a small number of peptide-based medicines have been approved by the U.S. FDA for specific medical conditions, many peptides sold for laboratory research have not been approved by the FDA. Research peptides are intended for scientific investigation and are not approved for human consumption.
Why are these products labeled "For Research Use Only"? expand_more
Research peptides have not completed the regulatory process required for FDA approval for human use. They are supplied for legitimate laboratory and scientific research and are not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.
